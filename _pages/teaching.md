Two decades at Intel inform how every concept is taught — from circuit fundamentals to system-level design. My goal is always the same: eliminate the gap between what students learn in the classroom and what the industry actually needs.

---

## Current Courses

**CSCE 312 — Computer Organization**
Undergraduate core. Datapath, control, memory hierarchy, pipelining, and I/O. Taught with real hardware examples drawn from Intel processor development.

**CSCE 410 — Operating Systems**
Processes, scheduling, virtual memory, file systems, and concurrency. Students build real components of an OS from scratch.

**CSCE 411 — Design & Analysis of Algorithms**
Graduate/senior undergraduate. Complexity theory, approximation algorithms, randomized algorithms, and NP-completeness.

---

## Special Topics Courses

**CSCE 489 — Hardware Security**
Processor vulnerabilities, hardware fuzzing, side-channel analysis, and secure architecture design. Covers TheHuzz methodology and real-world hardware CVEs.

**CSCE 689 — Machine Learning for CAD**
Graduate seminar. ML applications in EDA: logic synthesis, functional verification, coverage prediction, LLM-based RTL pipelines. Follows the research frontier directly.
