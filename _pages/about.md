I am the **Zachry Teaching Professor of Practice** in the [Department of Computer Science and Engineering](https://engineering.tamu.edu/cse/) at [Texas A&M University](https://www.tamu.edu), where I joined in 2014 as the department's first professor of practice after **20 years at Intel Corporation** — spanning eight generations of CPU development, including the Pentium family and Xeon Phi processors.

My research sits at the intersection of **hardware verification**, **secure computing**, **machine learning for CAD**, and most recently, **agentic AI energy measurement**. On the verification side, my group applies LLMs and multi-agent pipelines to automate RTL bug synthesis, waveform triage, and coverage closure — problems that have resisted automation for decades. On the energy side, we developed **A-LEMS** and introduced **EpG (Energy per Successful Goal)** and **OOI (Orchestration Overhead Index)** — the first goal-level energy accounting framework for agentic AI systems, showing that orchestration structure, not inference compute, is the primary determinant of energy cost in agentic workloads.

I am affiliated with the [Veritas Lab](https://veritas.cse.tamu.edu) and the [Institute for Engineering Education & Innovation (IEEI)](https://ieei.tamu.edu) at TAMU. In the classroom, I bring the same engineering precision I developed at Intel — bridging the gap between deep industry practice and academic rigor.

**I am looking for motivated PhD and MS students** interested in hardware verification, AI for chip design, and agentic AI systems. If your work touches any of these areas, reach out with your CV.
